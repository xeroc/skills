# Crunchbase Platform Guide

## 1. Platform Overview

Crunchbase is the world's most comprehensive database of companies, startups, funding information, and key industry professionals. Founded in 2007 by Michael Arrington as a supplement to TechCrunch, it originally tracked startups featured in TechCrunch articles. In September 2010, AOL acquired the platform as part of its broader acquisition of the TechCrunch network.

Today, Crunchbase operates as an independent company and serves as the go-to resource for entrepreneurs, investors, researchers, and business professionals seeking company intelligence. The platform hosts over 640,000 company profiles with millions of data points, making it an essential tool for anyone in the startup ecosystem.

### What Crunchbase Does

Crunchbase provides detailed information about:
- Company profiles with comprehensive business data
- Funding rounds from pre-seed to IPO
- Investor and VC firm profiles
- Key people and leadership information
- Market trends and industry insights
- Acquisition and merger data
- News and press coverage
- Financial metrics and growth indicators

### How It Works

The platform operates on a crowdsourced model where entrepreneurs, investors, and industry professionals contribute and verify data. Users can create personal and company profiles, track funding activities, research competitors, and connect with potential partners or investors.

Crunchbase generates revenue through multiple subscription tiers, providing basic information for free while offering advanced analytics, CRM integration, and detailed market intelligence through paid plans.

## 4. Submission Process

### Step 1: Create Personal Account

**URL**: https://www.crunchbase.com/register

**Requirements**:
- Valid email address
- Social media authentication (LinkedIn strongly recommended)
- Basic personal information

**Process**:
1. Visit Crunchbase homepage
2. Click "Sign Up" in upper right corner
3. Choose registration method (LinkedIn, Facebook, or email)
4. Complete profile verification
5. Verify email address

### Step 2: Create Company Profile

**URL**: Navigate to "Create Profile" under "Explore" in left sidebar

**Profile Creation Form Fields**:

**Basic Information**:
- Company Name (legally registered name)
- Company Description (category selection from dropdown)
- Also Known As (DBA or aliases)
- Company Type (for-profit/non-profit)
- Founded Date
- Closed Date (if applicable)

**Contact Information**:
- Company Website URL
- LinkedIn Company URL
- Twitter Company URL
- Contact Email (monitored business address)
- Phone Number (business line, not personal)
- Headquarters Address (professional address, not personal)

**Business Details**:
- Number of Employees (include founders)
- Categories (multiple selections allowed)
- Full Description (2-10,000 characters)

**Advanced Information**:
- Founders (link to existing profiles or create new)
- Funding Rounds (detailed funding history)
- Acquisitions (if applicable)
- IPO Information (if applicable)
- Featured Employees
- Board Members and Advisors
- Sub-Organizations
- News and Press Coverage

### Step 3: Profile Submission

**Submission Process**:
1. Complete all required fields
2. Review information for accuracy
3. Click "UPDATE" to submit
4. Confirm profile type (company/investment firm/school)
5. Profile goes live immediately with basic information
6. Advanced fields may require verification

**Processing Time**:
- Basic profile: Live immediately
- Enhanced data: 24-48 hours for verification
- Funded company data: May require additional verification

### Step 4: Claiming and Verification

**Claiming Process**:
- Search for existing profile of your company
- Click "Claim This Profile" if it exists
- Verify ownership through business email
- Complete verification process

**Verification Requirements**:
- Business email from company domain
- Social media account authentication
- May require additional documentation for larger companies

## 5. Requirements

### Technical Requirements

**Account Prerequisites**:
- Valid email address
- Social media account (LinkedIn, Facebook, or Twitter)
- Verified social media profile

**Content Guidelines**:
- Content must provide unique information about company
- Cannot be used as marketing tool to sell products
- Must maintain professional tone and accurate information
- No strictly personal information

**Image Requirements**:
- Profile Image: Minimum 100x100 pixels
- Maximum file size: 2MB
- Supported formats: JPG, PNG, GIF
- Cropping must be completed prior to upload

### Business Requirements

**Eligibility Criteria**:
- Legally registered business entity
- Active business operations
- Professional business address (no personal addresses)
- Business contact information

**Documentation Needed**:
- Business registration information
- Professional email address
- Company website (strongly recommended)
- Founder and key personnel information

**Restricted Content**:
- No purely personal profiles in company section
- No unregistered business ideas or concepts
- No marketing or promotional content disguised as company info
- No false or misleading information

### Data Quality Standards

**Verification Requirements**:
- All information must be accurate and verifiable
- Funding amounts must be documented
- Key personnel must be real individuals
- Company milestones must be factual

**Ongoing Maintenance**:
- Regular profile updates required
- Funding announcements should be timely
- Personnel changes should be updated
- Company status changes must be reflected

## 8. Common Mistakes / Gotchas

### Profile Creation Mistakes

**Personal Information Errors**:
- Using personal phone numbers or addresses
- Mixing personal LinkedIn with company information
- Including personal email addresses as company contact

**Content Quality Issues**:
- Writing marketing copy instead of factual descriptions
- Leaving key fields blank or incomplete
- Using inconsistent company names across platforms
- Including unverifiable or inflated metrics

**Technical Errors**:
- Poor quality images or logos
- Incorrect image sizing and cropping
- Broken website links
- Inconsistent social media linking

### Data Management Mistakes

**Funding Information Errors**:
- Inflating or misrepresenting funding amounts
- Including informal investments as formal rounds
- Wrong investor attribution or involvement
- Incorrect funding dates or valuations

**Personnel Mistakes**:
- Adding people without their permission
- Incorrect title or role attributions
- Linking to personal profiles inappropriately
- Outdated team information

**Business Information Errors**:
- Wrong company type designation (for-profit vs non-profit)
- Incorrect employee counts
- Outdated business addresses
- Wrong industry categorization

### Strategic Mistakes

**Premature Profile Creation**:
- Creating profile before business is legally registered
- Adding unformed business concepts
- Incomplete business model or unclear value proposition

**Maintenance Neglect**:
- Failing to update profile after major changes
- Ignoring funding announcement updates
- Not responding to profile inquiries
- Allowing outdated information to persist

**Over-Marketing**:
- Using profile as sales tool rather than information source
- Promotional language that violates content guidelines
- Excessive self-promotion in descriptions

### Platform Limitation Gotchas

**Edit Restrictions**:
- Certain fields can only be edited by Crunchbase staff
- Funding information may require verification
- Profile deletion requires staff intervention
- Historical data cannot always be modified

**Verification Challenges**:
- Social media authentication required for editing
- Business email verification for claiming profiles
- Additional documentation may be required for large companies
- Funding verification can be time-consuming
