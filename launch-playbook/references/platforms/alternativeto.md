# AlternativeTo Launch Platform Guide

## 1. Platform Overview

**AlternativeTo** is a crowdsourced platform that helps users discover alternatives to existing software and apps. Founded as a community-driven directory, it serves as the go-to destination for people looking to replace or find competitors to existing tools.

- **URL:** [alternativeto.net](https://alternativeto.net)
- **Category:** Software discovery and comparison platform
- **Founded:** 2009
- **Type:** Community-driven, moderated submission platform
- **Primary Function:** Connecting users with alternative software solutions across all categories

The platform operates on a simple premise: when users search for "alternatives to [software name]," AlternativeTo appears prominently in search results, making it a powerful organic discovery channel for startups.

## 3. Who Should Use This

**Perfect for:**
- **SaaS startups** positioned as alternatives to existing tools
- **Software products** with clear competitive positioning
- **Developer tools** and programming software
- **Productivity apps** competing with established players
- **Open-source projects** offering alternatives to proprietary software
- **Niche tools** that solve specific problems better than incumbents

**Especially effective if:**
- Your product is a direct competitor or alternative to a well-known tool
- You offer a free or more affordable version of existing paid software
- You have unique features that differentiate from competitors
- Your product targets developers, tech professionals, or productivity users

**Less suitable for:**
- Completely novel products with no clear alternatives
- Consumer apps without technical focus
- Products still in very early development
- Services rather than software tools

## 4. Submission Process

### Step-by-Step Submission:

1. **Create Account**
   - Visit [alternativeto.net](https://alternativeto.net)
   - Click "Sign up" in top right corner
   - Complete simple registration process

2. **Access Submission Form**
   - Log in and click on your User icon (top right)
   - Select "Suggest new application" from dropdown menu

3. **Complete 4-Step Form:**

   **Step 1: Name and Main Purpose**
   - Enter your application name
   - Write a clear, concise description of what your software does
   - Focus on the primary problem it solves

   **Step 2: Grab Data from External Sources**
   - Optional step for apps listed in Mac/iOS app stores
   - Skip if not applicable to your product

   **Step 3: Main Data (Critical Section)**
   - **Platforms:** Select all platforms your software supports
   - **License:** Choose appropriate license type (Free, Freemium, Paid, Open Source)
   - **Price:** Enter accurate pricing information
   - **Tags:** Add generous, relevant tags (these drive internal discovery)
   - **Description:** Detailed explanation of features and benefits
   - **Screenshots/Images:** Upload high-quality visuals

   **Step 4: Suggest Alternatives**
   - List apps your software is an alternative to
   - Be generous with suggestions to increase internal cross-references
   - This step is crucial for discovery and ranking

4. **Submit for Review**
   - Review all information for accuracy
   - Submit application for moderation approval
   - Typical approval time: 1-2 days

## 5. Requirements

- **Feature list:** Clear list of key capabilities

- Professional presentation (no obvious typos or low-quality images)

## 6. Strategy & Tips

### Optimization Strategies:

**Maximize Tag Usage:**
- Use all relevant tags - they drive internal discovery
- Include feature-based tags (e.g., "collaboration," "real-time," "API")
- Add category tags (e.g., "project management," "design," "analytics")
- Don't overstuff with irrelevant tags

**Alternative Mapping:**
- List ALL legitimate competitors, not just major ones
- Include both direct and indirect alternatives
- Add open-source alternatives if your product is commercial
- Be generous - more alternatives = more cross-references = better visibility

**Description Best Practices:**
- Lead with your unique value proposition
- Mention key differentiators from alternatives
- Include specific use cases and target audience
- Keep it scannable with clear benefits
- Avoid marketing fluff - focus on functionality

**Timing Strategy:**
- Submit after you have a functional product (not during development)
- Best to submit alongside other directory submissions for compound effect
- No specific timing advantages - consistent discovery over time

### Post-Submission Tactics:

**Continuous Improvement:**
- Regularly add newly discovered alternatives using the "add alternative" link
- Update your listing when you add major features
- Monitor and respond to user comments/reviews
- Keep pricing and platform information current

**Community Engagement:**
- Respond professionally to any user feedback
- Engage with users who comment on your listing
- Consider featuring your AlternativeTo listing in other marketing

## 8. Common Mistakes / Gotchas

### Submission Rejections:
- **Incomplete information:** Missing key details like pricing or platform support
- **Poor quality assets:** Low-resolution logos or unprofessional screenshots
- **Misleading descriptions:** Overpromising features or inaccurate claims
- **Too early submission:** Products still in development without functional demos
- **Duplicate submissions:** Multiple listings for the same product

### Post-Submission Issues:
- **Insufficient alternatives mapping:** Missing obvious competitors limits cross-discovery
- **Outdated information:** Not updating pricing, features, or availability
- **Ignoring user feedback:** Not responding to legitimate user comments or questions
- **Tag stuffing:** Using irrelevant tags that don't match your product

### Strategic Errors:
- **One-and-done approach:** Submitting and forgetting, rather than maintaining the listing
- **Poor positioning:** Not clearly articulating what makes your product different
- **Generic descriptions:** Using the same description as on your website rather than optimizing for alternative-seekers

### Technical Issues:
- **Website availability:** Ensure your website is accessible when submitted for review
- **Broken links:** Verify all submitted URLs are functional
- **Regional restrictions:** Not noting geographic limitations of your software
