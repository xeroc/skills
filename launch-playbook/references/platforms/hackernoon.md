# HackerNoon Platform Guide
*Comprehensive startup launch and awareness platform guide*

## 1. Platform Overview

HackerNoon is a major technology publishing platform founded in 2016 by David Smooke (CEO) and Linh Dao Smooke (COO) under ArtMap Inc. Originally launched as a Medium publication, HackerNoon became an independent platform in July 2019 when they built their own content management system and publishing platform.

**Core Mission:** "How hackers start their afternoons" - HackerNoon is built for technologists to read, write, learn, and publish technology content.

**Platform Philosophy:**
- Open and international community of writers and readers
- Free editorial story improvements by human editors
- Paywall-free and pop-up-free reading experience
- Stories written by real tech professionals, not generic content farms
- Focus on first-person perspective over breaking news

**Content Focus Areas:**
- Software development and programming
- Artificial intelligence and machine learning
- Blockchain and cryptocurrency
- Cybersecurity
- Startup advice and entrepreneurship
- Product management and marketing
- Engineering and architecture
- Technology trends and analysis

## 4. Submission Process

**Primary Submission Portal:** https://app.hackernoon.com (requires account creation)

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Create Account:** Sign up at https://auth.hackernoon.com/
2. **Draft Creation:** Log in and click "Start Writing"
3. **Content Development:**
   - Add compelling title (80 characters or less, Title Case preferred)
   - Upload feature image (required - never submit without image)
   - Write comprehensive article (aim for high read time)
   - Add 5-8 relevant tags for SEO optimization
   - Format with subheadings, bullet points, proper paragraph breaks

4. **Story Settings & Submission:**
   - Configure story settings (category, tags, author bio)
   - Submit story for editorial review via "Submit for Review" button
   - Wait for editorial team response (3-4 business days median)

**Alternative Submission Method:**
- Email: stories@hackernoon.com (still available but lower priority)
- Note: Contribute.HackerNoon.com platform is the prioritized review channel

**Editorial Review Process:**
- All stories reviewed and improved by human editorial team
- Stories may be published, rejected, or returned for revisions
- Editorial team provides feedback on improvements needed
- Some stories selected for homepage featuring based on read time and engagement

**Content Requirements:**
- Minimum viable length: Substantial content (aim for 1000+ words for best performance)
- Original content preferred (republishing allowed with proper guidelines)
- Tech industry focus required
- First-person narrative encouraged
- Must include proper attribution for sources and quotes

## 5. Requirements

**Content Quality Standards:**

**Topic Requirements:**
- Must be technology-related (software, hardware, AI, blockchain, startups, etc.)
- Original angle or fresh perspective required
- In-depth analysis preferred over surface-level coverage
- First-person experience and lessons learned highly valued

**Technical Requirements:**
- Feature image mandatory (original preferred, Unsplash acceptable)
- Proper formatting with headers, bullet points, numbered lists
- Natural hyperlink integration (no bare URLs)
- 5-8 strategic tags matching content and target keywords
- Headlines under 80 characters, in Title Case

**Writing Standards:**
- Error-free grammar and spelling (stories may be rejected for poor editing)
- Clear, engaging introductory paragraph that hooks readers
- Logical flow and structure throughout
- Include author credentials and perspective
- Cite sources and provide attribution
- No clickbait headlines

**Prohibited Content:**
- Gambling and narcotics content
- CBD and adult content
- "SEO company in [city]" type spam
- Essay writing services
- Pure backlink farming attempts
- Content unrelated to technology industry

**Author Requirements:**
- Real person with genuine tech industry experience
- Author bio explaining credentials and perspective
- Professional headshot or avatar
- Consistent writing style aligned with tech professional audience

**Backlink Policy:**
- Very stringent backlink requirements
- Links must be natural and add value to content
- No excessive self-promotion
- Brand-as-author accounts available for companies wanting to include backlinks

## 6. Strategy & Tips

**Content Strategy for Maximum Impact:**

**Headline Optimization:**
- Test headlines with one specific person in mind (friend, colleague, mentor)
- Ensure headlines pass the "So what?" test
- Include target keywords naturally
- Create curiosity without being clickbait
- Consider emotional appeal along with logical interest

**SEO Optimization:**
- Use all 8 available tags strategically
- Target keywords your audience actually searches for
- Include target keywords in headline and throughout content naturally
- Link to relevant internal HackerNoon content when appropriate
- Optimize for featured snippets with clear, structured information

**Engagement Tactics:**
- Focus on read time over quick engagement metrics
- Break up long sections with images, quotes, code blocks
- Use subheadings to make content scannable
- Include original graphs, infographics, or data visualizations
- Embed relevant videos to provide multiple perspectives

**Distribution Amplification:**
- Submit during weekdays for better editorial attention
- Promote across your own social channels after publication
- Engage with comments and build community
- Cross-post to relevant subreddits and tech communities
- Share in relevant Slack communities and Discord servers

**Positioning for Homepage Feature:**
- Target trending topics with unique angles
- Focus on evergreen content that ages well
- Write comprehensive guides that become reference material
- Share contrarian but well-reasoned perspectives
- Include actionable insights readers can implement

**Series and Follow-up Strategy:**
- Create content series to build following
- Reference previous articles to drive traffic to older content
- Build on trending topics with deeper analysis
- Create "lessons learned" content from startup experiences

## 8. Common Mistakes / Gotchas

**Content Mistakes That Lead to Rejection:**

**Generic Business Content:**
- Writing general entrepreneurship advice without tech focus
- Submitting content that could apply to any industry
- Focusing on business fundamentals rather than tech-specific insights
- Creating content that reads like a press release

**Technical Writing Errors:**
- Submitting articles without feature images
- Poor formatting with no subheadings or structure
- Including bare URLs instead of natural hyperlinks
- Overusing company/product names without providing value
- Writing clickbait headlines that don't deliver on promises

**SEO and Tagging Mistakes:**
- Using irrelevant tags to try to game the system
- Keyword stuffing instead of natural integration
- Choosing tags based on popularity rather than content relevance
- Not utilizing all 8 available tag slots

**Editorial Red Flags:**
- Excessive self-promotion disguised as educational content
- Lack of author credentials or perspective
- Poor grammar and spelling that suggests low effort
- Republishing content without following proper guidelines
- Including affiliate links or obvious sales pitches

**Timing and Process Errors:**
- Submitting incomplete drafts for review
- Not responding to editorial feedback promptly
- Expecting immediate publication without editorial review
- Submitting on weekends or holidays when editorial team is less active

**Community Engagement Mistakes:**
- Not engaging with comments on published articles
- Failing to promote content after publication
- Not building relationships within the HackerNoon community
- Treating HackerNoon as a one-time publication rather than ongoing relationship
