# Launchboard Platform Guide

## 1. Platform Overview

**Platform:** LaunchBoard  
**URL:** https://www.launchboard.dev  
**Creator:** Roubal Sehgal (@roubalsehgal)  
**Category:** Weekly Product Launch Platform / Community-Driven Discovery  

LaunchBoard is a curated product launch platform that operates on a weekly launch cycle, specifically every Monday at 03:30:00 UTC (09:00 AM IST). Unlike the intense daily competition of Product Hunt, LaunchBoard provides a more focused, less crowded launch environment where products compete throughout an entire week.

The platform focuses on helping indie makers, solo founders, and startups gain genuine visibility through a structured weekly launch cycle. Products are batched together each Monday with a maximum of 25 products per week, ensuring each product receives proper attention and community engagement.

LaunchBoard positions itself as a "DR50+ backlinks" platform that provides permanent dofollow backlinks, targeted traffic from makers and founders, and genuine feedback from real users actively building products. The weekly format reduces the stress and timing constraints of daily launch platforms while maintaining community engagement throughout the week.

## 4. Submission Process

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. **Account Creation**
   - Visit https://www.launchboard.dev
   - Click "Submit Your Product" (triggers sign-in if not authenticated)
   - Register with email or "Continue with Google" option
   - Complete basic profile setup

2. **Product Submission Form**
   - **Product Name:** Clear, concise product name
   - **Product Description:** Detailed explanation of features and benefits
   - **Website URL:** Live, functional product website (required)
   - **Product Image/Logo:** High-quality logo or product screenshot
   - **Category Selection:** Choose from available categories (AI, Marketing, Productivity, etc.)
   - **Maker Information:** Brief bio and contact details
   - **Tags:** Relevant category and feature tags

3. **Queue Assignment**
   - Products automatically assigned to next available weekly batch
   - Maximum 25 products per week ensures quality curation
   - Review process typically completes within 24-48 hours

4. **Launch Day (Monday 03:30 UTC)**
   - All approved products go live simultaneously
   - Products compete based on upvotes and community engagement
   - Week-long voting period with peak activity Tuesday-Wednesday
   - Winners determined Sunday, featured prominently on platform

**Submission URL:** https://www.launchboard.dev (main submit button)  
**Review Timeline:** 24 hours for premium submissions, 1-2 business days for free submissions with badge verification

## 6. Strategy & Tips

**Pre-Launch Strategy (2-3 weeks before):**
- **Community Building:** Engage with LaunchBoard's existing launches and makers
- **Email List Building:** Build list of supporters who can vote on launch day
- **Content Preparation:** Prepare week-long content calendar for sustained promotion
- **Network Activation:** Coordinate with fellow makers, co-founders, and industry contacts
- **Monday Planning:** Plan around Monday 03:30 UTC launch time (consider time zones)

**Launch Week Execution:**
- **Monday (Launch Day):** Coordinate immediate team and close network push
- **Tuesday-Wednesday (Peak Voting):** Maximum promotion across all channels
- **Thursday-Friday:** Maintain momentum with fresh content and engagement
- **Weekend:** Final push for votes, thank supporters, engage with comments
- **Sunday:** Results determined, celebrate or learn from experience

**Content Strategy for Week-Long Cycle:**
- Day 1-2: "Just launched on LaunchBoard" announcement content
- Day 3-4: Behind-the-scenes content, maker stories, feature highlights
- Day 5-6: User testimonials, early feedback, problem-solving content
- Day 7: Final vote push, gratitude posts, results sharing

**Community Engagement Best Practices:**
- Vote and comment on other products launching the same week
- Share insights and feedback genuinely (build relationships, not transactions)
- Participate in platform discussions beyond your launch week
- Support other makers consistently to build reciprocal relationships

**Timing Considerations:**
- Monday 03:30 UTC = Sunday 10:30 PM EST / Monday 9:00 AM IST
- Plan team availability around launch timing
- Consider global audience reach during peak voting periods
- Avoid launching during major tech announcements or holidays

## 8. Common Mistakes / Gotchas

**Submission Mistakes:**
- **"Last-Minute Submissions:"** Submitting too close to desired launch week - platform queues products in advance
- **"Incomplete Asset Preparation:"** Low-quality logos, missing descriptions, or broken website links
- **"Generic Descriptions:"** Using template descriptions instead of maker-specific value propositions
- **"Category Misalignment:"** Choosing wrong category reduces discoverability by target audience

**Launch Week Mistakes:**
- **"Monday-Only Mindset:"** Treating launch like Product Hunt's 24-hour cycle instead of week-long opportunity
- **"Ignoring Other Launches:"** Failing to engage with other products in same launch week
- **"Inconsistent Engagement:"** Strong Monday push followed by abandonment for rest of week
- **"Time Zone Confusion:"** Missing optimal engagement windows due to UTC timing miscalculation

**Strategic Mistakes:**
- **"Traffic Expectations:"** Expecting Product Hunt-level traffic from smaller platform (manage expectations)
- **"One-Platform Strategy:"** Using LaunchBoard as only launch platform instead of multi-platform approach
- **"Relationship Extraction:"** Treating community purely transactionally instead of building genuine connections
- **"Post-Launch Abandonment:"** Disappearing from platform after launch instead of ongoing community participation

**Technical Gotchas:**
- **"UTC Timing Complexity:"** Monday 03:30 UTC affects different global regions differently - plan accordingly
- **"Mobile Experience:"** Many voters browse on mobile - ensure mobile-optimized product pages
- **"Video Autoplay Issues:"** Demo videos may not autoplay with sound - design accordingly
- **"Comment Notification Setup:"** Missing comment replies due to notification settings

**Community Dynamics:**
- **"Maker Audience Assumption:"** Platform skews heavily toward developer and maker audience - adjust messaging
- **"Weekly Competition Intensity:"** While less intense than daily platforms, weekly competition still requires sustained effort
- **"Small Community Effect:"** Smaller community means individual relationships matter more than mass appeal
