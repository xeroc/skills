# Firsto Platform Guide

## 1. Platform Overview

**Firsto** is a fair product launch platform designed to give every product real visibility, positioning itself as a direct alternative to Product Hunt. Built on the open-source Open-Launch foundation, Firsto focuses on discoverability over virality, ensuring every launch gets seen rather than buried in endless feeds or algorithmic bias.

- **URL**: https://firsto.co
- **Twitter**: @FirstoContact
- **Category**: Product Launch Platform / Startup Directory
- **Positioning**: "Product Hunt Alternative" - Fair visibility for all launches
- **Unique Value Proposition**: Daily launches with guaranteed visibility - only 2 homepage spots per day
- **Launch Date**: May 2025 (relatively new platform, significant growth since launch)
- **Technical Foundation**: Built on Open-Launch (open-source project), heavily modified and enhanced
- **Company**: erwa tech llc (based on status page: https://firsto.betteruptime.com/)
- **System Status**: Real-time monitoring available at https://firsto.betteruptime.com/

### Platform Philosophy

Firsto operates on the principle that "every product deserves to be seen — not just the projects that already have a big audience." This philosophy drives their core feature: guaranteed visibility for every submission through their daily launch system and comprehensive discovery mechanisms.

**Key Differentiators:**
- Limited daily launches (maximum 2 per day) ensuring each gets attention
- Long-term discoverability through searchable project pages
- Winners recognition system (daily, weekly, monthly)
- No algorithmic bias against smaller products
- Built-in SEO benefits with dofollow backlinks
- Comprehensive catalog system for historical browsing

## 4. Submission Process & Requirements

### Step-by-Step Submission Guide

**Account Setup:**
1. **Registration**: Create account at https://firsto.co/sign-up
   - Email verification required
   - Profile setup with maker information
   - Social media links recommended but optional

2. **Access Submission Portal**: Navigate to https://firsto.co/projects/submit
   - Must be logged in to access submission form
   - Choose between Free and Premium launch options

**Submission Form Structure:**
The platform uses a horizontal step layout to minimize scrolling and provide clear completion path:

**Step 1: Basic Product Information**
- **Product Name**: Clear, descriptive name (avoid marketing fluff)
- **Product URL**: Working website or demo link (required)
- **Category Selection**: Choose most relevant category from platform taxonomy
- **Launch Type**: Free (queue system) or Premium ($19 for priority)

**Step 2: Visual Assets**
- **Logo Upload**: High-resolution product logo (minimum 512x512px recommended)
- **Multiple Images**: Support for carousel of product screenshots/images
- **Featured Image**: Primary promotional image (1200x630px optimal)
- **Video Support**: Optional YouTube/Vimeo embed for product demos

**Step 3: Product Description**
- **Tagline**: Concise value proposition (recommended 10-15 words)
- **Detailed Description**: Full markdown support including:
  - Formatted text with headings, bold, italics
  - Code blocks for technical details
  - Links to documentation or resources
  - Lists and structured content
- **Live Preview**: Real-time markdown preview in submission interface

**Step 4: Additional Details**
- **Maker Information**: Founder/creator details and background
- **Tags**: Relevant keywords for discoverability (helps with categorization)
- **Social Media Links**: Twitter, LinkedIn, GitHub, personal website
- **Pricing Information**: Free, freemium, paid tiers with specific pricing
- **Exclusive Offers**: Discount codes or special promotions for Firsto users

**Step 5: Launch Scheduling**
- **Calendar Interface**: Visual date picker showing available launch slots
- **Launch Time**: All launches occur at 8:00 UTC daily
- **Availability**: Only 2 products can launch per day (guaranteed visibility)
- **Premium Priority**: Paid submissions get first choice of preferred dates

### Review & Approval Process

**Timeline:**
- **Submission Review**: 24-72 hours for approval
- **Content Moderation**: Quality check for appropriateness and completeness
- **Launch Confirmation**: Email notification with confirmed launch date
- **Pre-Launch Notifications**: Reminders sent before scheduled launch

**Approval Criteria:**
- Working product URL with functional demo/website
- Clear value proposition in description
- Appropriate visual assets (logo and screenshots)
- Complete submission form with all required fields
- Product fits within platform's focus areas

## 5. Platform Requirements & Standards

### Technical Requirements

**Essential Elements:**
- **Working Product**: Functional website, app, or demo environment
- **Clear URL Structure**: Professional domain preferred over subdomain
- **Mobile Responsiveness**: Product should work across devices
- **Loading Performance**: Fast-loading pages for good user experience

### Content Requirements

**Required Content:**
- **Product Name**: Descriptive, memorable, searchable name
- **Value Proposition**: Clear explanation of problem solved
- **Target Audience**: Specific user types or use cases
- **Features Overview**: Key functionality and benefits
- **Pricing Model**: Transparent pricing structure
- **Contact Information**: Ways for users to reach founder/team

**Visual Asset Standards:**
- **Logo Quality**: Vector preferred, minimum 512x512px for raster
- **Screenshot Standards**: Clean, well-lit, showing actual product interface
- **Image Formats**: JPG, PNG, WebP supported
- **File Size Limits**: Reasonable limits to ensure fast loading
- **Professional Presentation**: Avoid pixelated or amateur-looking visuals

### Writing Standards

**Description Best Practices:**
- **Problem-Solution Focus**: Lead with user pain point, follow with solution
- **Specific Benefits**: Quantified results where possible
- **Technical Accuracy**: Honest representation of current capabilities
- **Clear Language**: Avoid jargon unless targeting technical audience
- **Call-to-Action**: Clear next steps for interested users

**Markdown Support Features:**
- Headers (H1-H6) for content structure
- Bold and italic text for emphasis
- Code blocks with syntax highlighting
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Links to external resources
- Block quotes for testimonials or important notes

## 9. Common Mistakes & Pitfalls

### Submission Phase Mistakes

**Content Quality Issues:**
- **Generic Descriptions**: Copying boilerplate text instead of crafting unique value propositions
- **Weak Visual Assets**: Using low-resolution logos, poor screenshots, or placeholder images
- **Incomplete Information**: Rushing through submission without filling all relevant fields
- **Wrong Category Selection**: Misclassifying product affects long-term discoverability
- **No Social Proof**: Failing to include testimonials, case studies, or user feedback

**Technical Preparation Failures:**
- **Broken Links**: Submitting with non-functional product URLs or demo links
- **Poor Performance**: Product crashes or loads slowly under increased traffic
- **Mobile Issues**: Desktop-only products losing mobile audience
- **Incomplete Product**: Launching MVP without core functionality working
- **No Analytics**: Missing opportunity to track launch traffic and conversions

### Launch Timing Issues

**Schedule Misalignment:**
- **Weekend Launches**: Significantly lower engagement on Saturdays/Sundays
- **Holiday Conflicts**: Launching during major holidays when audience is unavailable
- **No Promotion Coordination**: Failing to align with email lists and social media
- **Wrong Time Zone Planning**: Not accounting for 8:00 UTC launch time in marketing
- **Rush Scheduling**: Not allowing enough time for approval and promotion preparation

**Communication Gaps:**
- **Single Channel Promotion**: Relying only on Firsto without cross-platform strategy
- **No Follow-Up**: Disappearing after launch instead of engaging with community
- **Missed Opportunities**: Not leveraging launch momentum for other marketing activities

### Post-Launch Missteps

**Engagement Problems:**
- **Passive Response**: Not replying to comments or feedback from community
- **One-Time Activity**: Treating launch as single event instead of ongoing relationship
- **No Community Building**: Missing opportunities to connect with other makers
- **Ignoring Feedback**: Not incorporating user suggestions for product improvement
- **No Success Sharing**: Failing to document and share results for future reference

**SEO Optimization Misses:**
- **Not Tracking Backlinks**: Missing valuable SEO data from launch
- **Static Product Pages**: Never updating information as product evolves
- **Poor Tag Strategy**: Using generic tags instead of specific, searchable keywords
- **No Long-Term Monitoring**: Not tracking ongoing organic discovery from search

### Platform-Specific Gotchas

**Expectations Management:**
- **Traffic Volume Assumptions**: Expecting massive traffic spikes like major platforms
- **Instant Success Mentality**: Not understanding that Firsto prioritizes quality over viral reach
- **Competition Comparison**: Comparing directly to Product Hunt without considering audience differences
- **Pricing Misconceptions**: Expecting premium features without paying for enhanced visibility

**Community Dynamics:**
- **Over-Promotion**: Too aggressive marketing instead of authentic community participation
- **Ignoring Platform Culture**: Not adapting to Firsto's collaborative, supportive atmosphere
- **No Reciprocal Engagement**: Not supporting other makers' launches and building relationships

## 10. Platform Comparison Context

### vs. Product Hunt

**Audience Differences:**
- **Product Hunt**: Broader, more consumer-focused audience with higher tourist traffic
- **Firsto**: More targeted, maker-focused community with higher intent to adopt new tools

**Launch Experience:**
- **Product Hunt**: High competition, algorithm-dependent, viral-or-invisible outcomes
- **Firsto**: Guaranteed visibility, collaborative atmosphere, sustained discoverability

**Conversion Quality:**
- **Product Hunt**: Higher volume, lower conversion rates, more passive browsers
- **Firsto**: Lower volume, higher conversion rates, more active evaluation

### vs. Hacker News

**Technical Focus:**
- **Hacker News**: Extremely technical audience, high scrutiny, developer-heavy
- **Firsto**: Broader maker audience, more welcoming to non-technical products

**Submission Process:**
- **Hacker News**: Simple but high rejection risk, community-driven moderation
- **Firsto**: Structured submission, professional moderation, predictable approval

### vs. Indie Hackers

**Community Style:**
- **Indie Hackers**: Revenue-focused, detailed progress sharing, long-form engagement
- **Firsto**: Product-focused, concise presentations, discovery-oriented

**Time Investment:**
- **Indie Hackers**: High community participation required over time
- **Firsto**: Moderate engagement, launch-focused rather than ongoing community building

### Strategic Platform Selection

**Choose Firsto When:**
- Product needs guaranteed visibility over viral potential
- Targeting makers, developers, and early adopters specifically
- Want sustainable discovery beyond single launch day
- Budget-conscious marketing (free or $19 vs hundreds elsewhere)
- International product seeking English-speaking audience
- Building portfolio of multiple product launches

**Choose Alternatives When:**
- Need massive traffic volume for consumer products
- Targeting non-technical, mainstream audiences
- Have existing large social media following to leverage
- Require specific industry expertise or B2B focus
- Building long-term community presence vs. product discovery
