# RankInPublic Platform Guide

## Platform Overview

**RankInPublic** is a community-driven startup launch platform that revolutionizes product discovery through competitive tournament brackets. Unlike traditional launch platforms where the biggest audience typically wins, RankInPublic creates fair 1v1 battles where startups compete for visibility through authentic community votes in a tournament-style elimination format.

- **Website:** https://rankinpublic.xyz
- **Founders:** Pablo Santana (@PabloSantanaT) and Antonio Escudero (@AntonioEscudero)
- **Category:** SaaS Launch Platform / Product Discovery / Competition-driven SEO Platform
- **Format:** Weekly tournament brackets (16 → 8 → 4 → 2 → 1 elimination format)
- **Launch Year:** 2025
- **Base:** Spain
- **Unique Value Proposition:** "Instead of a leaderboard where the biggest audience wins, products compete in 1v1 tournament brackets. Community votes. Best product advances."

## Submission Process

**Step-by-step submission process:**

1. **Visit the Platform:** Navigate to https://rankinpublic.xyz
2. **Submit Your Product:** Complete the submission form with required information
3. **Provide Required Assets:**
   - Product name and compelling description
   - Live website URL (functional and responsive required)
   - High-quality product logo and screenshots
   - Category selection for proper tournament placement
4. **Tournament Assignment:** Products are grouped into tournaments (typically 16 products per bracket)
5. **Tournament Launch:** Daily elimination rounds over 5 days (16→8→4→2→1)
6. **Active Promotion:** Share tournament participation with your network for community votes
7. **Results and Benefits:** Win or lose, gain exposure, backlinks, and community feedback

**Timeline Details:**
- **Submission:** Rolling submissions accepted continuously
- **Tournament Duration:** 5 days total (daily elimination rounds)
- **Tournament Frequency:** Weekly tournaments with varying sizes
- **Voting Period:** Daily voting windows for each round
- **Results:** Tournament winners announced after final round

## Common Mistakes / Gotchas

**Platform-Specific Issues:**

1. **Inadequate Landing Page:**
   - **Problem:** Tournament success heavily depends on visual appeal and immediate comprehension
   - **Solution:** Invest in professional design before submitting
   - **Example:** Products with unclear value propositions struggle in 1v1 comparisons

2. **Passive Tournament Participation:**
   - **Problem:** Relying solely on platform traffic without active promotion
   - **Solution:** Treat tournament as a 5-day marketing campaign
   - **Critical:** Platform traffic alone won't guarantee advancement through brackets

3. **Wrong Tournament Timing:**
   - **Problem:** Submitting when you can't actively promote during the competition
   - **Solution:** Align tournament timing with your marketing calendar
   - **Consideration:** Weekend vs. weekday tournaments may have different engagement levels

4. **Poor Category Classification:**
   - **Problem:** Misclassifying your product reduces relevant audience exposure
   - **Solution:** Choose the most specific, accurate category for your product type
   - **Impact:** Wrong category means competing against unrelated products

5. **Incomplete Submission Assets:**
   - **Problem:** Missing or low-quality logos, screenshots, or descriptions
   - **Solution:** Treat submission like a mini Product Hunt launch - high production value
   - **Standard:** Professional assets are table stakes for tournament success

**Quality Considerations:**
- Platform launched in 2025, so track record is still developing compared to established platforms
- Smaller audience than Product Hunt means success depends more on product quality than hype
- Tournament format is fair but requires active participation to maximize benefits
- SEO benefits are long-term; immediate traffic depends on your promotion efforts
