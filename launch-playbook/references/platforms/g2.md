# G2 Platform Guide

## Platform Overview

**What it is:** G2 (formerly G2 Crowd) is the world's largest and most trusted software marketplace and peer review platform for B2B software. It enables users to discover, research, and evaluate business software through authentic peer reviews and comparative analysis.

**Who runs it:** Founded in 2012 by Godard Abel and team (former BigMachines employees), headquartered in Chicago, Illinois.

**URL:** https://www.g2.com/

**Category:** Software review platform, B2B marketplace, and SaaS discovery engine.

## Key Stats

**Domain Rating:** High-authority domain (90+ DR estimated based on traffic patterns)

**Monthly Traffic:** Millions of monthly visitors (exact figures proprietary, but consistently ranks as top software discovery platform)

**Audience Size:** 
- 5+ million software professionals using the platform
- 1.5 million+ verified user reviews
- 150,000+ software products listed
- Used by 80%+ of Fortune 500 companies for software decisions

**Pricing:**
- **Free Plan:** Basic listing, limited features
- **G2 Brand Starter:** ~$15,000/year (list price, often negotiated down to ~$9,000-10,000)
- **G2 Brand Professional:** ~$25,000-40,000/year  
- **G2 Brand Enterprise:** $50,000+/year
- **Add-ons:** Buyer Intent data (~$36,000/year), Review Growth (~$36,000/year)

## Who Should Use This

**Ideal for:**
- B2B SaaS companies (any size)
- Enterprise software providers
- Niche category leaders seeking visibility
- Companies with satisfied customers willing to write reviews
- Businesses targeting software buyers who research before purchasing

**Best product types:**
- Business software and SaaS tools
- Enterprise applications
- Productivity tools
- Marketing technology
- Development tools
- HR and operations software

**NOT ideal for:**
- Consumer-focused products
- Physical products
- Services without software components
- Companies unwilling to actively collect reviews

## Submission Process

### Step 1: Initial Setup
1. Visit https://sell.g2.com/ or contact G2 directly
2. Create vendor account with business email
3. Provide company information and product details
4. Verify business legitimacy (business email domain required)

### Step 2: Product Profile Creation
1. Complete product information form
2. Upload company logo and product screenshots
3. Write compelling product description
4. Select appropriate software categories (G2 determines final categorization)
5. Add integrations, supported platforms, and technical details

### Step 3: Review and Launch
1. G2 reviews submission for completeness and accuracy
2. Profile goes live (typically 1-2 weeks)
3. Begin review collection immediately upon launch

## Strategy & Tips

### Pre-Launch Strategy:
1. **Identify your category** - Research where competitors are listed
2. **Audit competitor profiles** - Note what works well in your category
3. **Prepare review collection plan** - Line up 5-10 happy customers ready to review
4. **Optimize for keywords** - Use terms your buyers search for

### Launch Strategy:
1. **Get first 5 reviews quickly** - Essential for credibility
2. **Focus on quality over quantity** - Detailed, authentic reviews perform better
3. **Respond to reviews** (paid plans only) - Shows engagement and care
4. **Monitor competitor activity** - Stay informed about category changes

### Long-term Strategy:
1. **Maintain steady review flow** - 2-3 new reviews per month ideal
2. **Target G2 seasonal campaigns** - Awards, reports, badges
3. **Leverage buyer intent data** (paid feature) - Identify prospects researching your category
4. **Cross-promote reviews** - Use G2 reviews in other marketing materials

## Common Mistakes / Gotchas

### Profile Setup Mistakes:
- **Incomplete product information** - Leads to lower search visibility
- **Poor quality screenshots** - Significantly impacts conversion rates
- **Generic product descriptions** - Fails to differentiate from competitors
- **Wrong category selection** - Reduces discoverability by target audience

### Review Collection Pitfalls:
- **Waiting too long to collect reviews** - Harder to get reviews from older customers
- **Not following up** - Single ask rarely generates reviews
- **Incentivizing reviews inappropriately** - G2 prohibits paid reviews
- **Focusing only on quantity** - Quality and recency matter more

### Platform Changes:
- **Free plan limitations increasing** - Many features now require paid plans
- **Review moderation tightening** - Legitimate reviews sometimes rejected
- **Competitor ads on profiles** - Free plans now show competitor alternatives prominently
- **Badge restrictions** - Many badge types now require paid plans

### SEO and Visibility:
- **Ignoring G2's Google ranking power** - G2 pages often rank highly in Google
- **Not optimizing for buyer search terms** - Missing key discovery opportunities
- **Neglecting profile maintenance** - Outdated information hurts credibility
