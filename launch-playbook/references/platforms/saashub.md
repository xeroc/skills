# SaaSHub - Platform Guide

## 1. Platform Overview

SaaSHub is an independent software marketplace and directory that has been helping software professionals discover and compare SaaS products since 2014. Founded by Stan, the platform serves as a trusted repository for software alternatives, reviews, and startup discovery.

- **URL:** https://www.saashub.com/
- **Founded:** 2014
- **Category:** SaaS Directory & Software Marketplace
- **Founder:** Stan (active in community engagement)
- **Primary Function:** Software discovery, comparison, and alternatives platform

SaaSHub positions itself as an objective, simple platform that serves as users' "first stop when researching for a new service to help you grow your business." Unlike launch platforms focused on short-term attention, SaaSHub is designed as a permanent directory for long-term discoverability.

## 2. Key Stats

- **Domain Rating:** 75-76 (High Authority)
- **Monthly Traffic:** 2-3 million visits
- **Link Type:** Do-follow backlinks
- **Audience:** Software professionals, startup founders, business decision-makers
- **Pricing:** Free submission
- **Approval Time:** Less than 7 days (often within 8 hours if founder comments)
- **Global Reach:** Primarily English-speaking markets

The platform receives hundreds of thousands of monthly visitors actively searching for software alternatives and new tools, making it one of the larger independent directories in the SaaS space.

## 3. Who Should Use This

**Perfect for:**
- SaaS, IaaS & PaaS products and services
- Most software products and apps
- Mobile apps with decent websites
- Websites/services that are leaders in specific niches (e.g., Airbnb)
- Products available in English
- Startups seeking long-term credibility and discoverability
- Founders wanting quality backlinks for SEO

**NOT suitable for:**
- Software development agencies (explicitly rejected)
- Non-English products

- Services that don't fit software/SaaS categories

## 4. Submission Process

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Visit the submission page:** Go to https://www.saashub.com/services/submit
2. **Read the guidelines carefully** before submitting
3. **Create an account** if you don't already have one
4. **Complete the submission form** with:
   - Product name and description
   - Website URL
   - Categories (check competitors for inspiration)
   - Competitor listings (REQUIRED - submission slowed without them)
   - Screenshots and assets
   - Pricing information
5. **Follow submission guidelines** to avoid rejection
6. **Wait for approval** (manual review process)
7. **Optional:** Comment on founder's Reddit posts for faster approval

### Alternative Method:
- Use SaaSHub's self-service tool at https://www.saashub.com/submit/list to submit to 100+ directories at once (launched by founder Stan in October 2024)

## 5. Requirements

### Essential Requirements:
- **Working product** with live website or app

- **High-quality screenshots** of your product interface

### Technical Requirements:

- Functional website (no broken links)
- Professional presentation
- Mobile-responsive design preferred

## 6. Strategy & Tips

### Best Practices:
- **List competitors:** Essential for faster approval and proper categorization
- **Choose relevant categories:** Study similar products to find the right fit
- **Write compelling descriptions:** Focus on value proposition and unique features
- **Provide quality screenshots:** Show actual product interface, not marketing graphics
- **Complete all fields:** Comprehensive listings perform better
- **Engage with community:** The founder is active on Reddit - engage authentically

- Approval typically within 7 days, often much faster

- SEO benefits compound over time

## 8. Common Mistakes / Gotchas

### Rejection Triggers:
- **No competitor list:** Submissions without competitors get deprioritized
- **Software agencies:** Explicitly not accepted
- **Poor quality submissions:** Incomplete information or broken links
- **Non-English products:** Only English products accepted
- **Generic descriptions:** Vague or promotional language instead of clear value props

### Performance Killers:
- **Incomplete listings:** Missing key information reduces discoverability
- **Poor screenshots:** Low-quality images hurt credibility
- **Ignoring guidelines:** Not following submission requirements
- **Set-and-forget approach:** Not maintaining or updating listings over time

### Quality Issues:
- **Limited user reviews:** Some listings have minimal feedback
- **Imperfect alternatives:** Suggested alternatives aren't always accurate matches
- **No day-one boost:** Unlike launch platforms, benefits accrue over time
